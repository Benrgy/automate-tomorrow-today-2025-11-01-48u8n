Cookies and Tracking Technologies

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, remember your preferences, and provide information to website owners. Cookies can be either "persistent" cookies (which remain on your device until you delete them) or "session" cookies (which are deleted when you close your browser).

Types of Cookies We Use

Essential Cookies:

These cookies are necessary for the basic functionality of our website and Services. They enable you to navigate our site and use its features. Without these cookies, some services cannot be provided.

Performance Cookies:

These cookies collect information about how you use our Services, such as which pages you visit and how long you spend on them. This information helps us improve the performance and functionality of our website.

Functionality Cookies:

These cookies remember your choices and preferences to provide a more personalized experience. They may remember your login information, language preferences, and other customizations.

Marketing Cookies:

These cookies are used to track your activity across websites and deliver targeted advertising. They help us understand which ads are effective and measure the success of our marketing campaigns.

Managing Your Cookie Preferences

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can typically find these settings in your browser's "Options," "Preferences," or "Settings" menu. You may be able to:

  • View what cookies have been set
  • Block cookies from being set in the future
  • Delete existing cookies
  • Set your browser to notify you when cookies are being set

Please note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality of our Services and your user experience.

Other Tracking Technologies

In addition to cookies, we may use other tracking technologies such as:

  • Web Beacons: Small transparent images embedded in web pages and emails that track whether a page has been viewed
  • Pixels: Tiny pieces of code that allow us to track user interactions and conversions
  • Local Storage: Technology that stores data on your device for website functionality and personalization
  • Analytics Tools: Third-party services that track and analyze user behavior on our website
